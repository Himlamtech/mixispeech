Abstract

Diffusion and flow-matching TTS systems produce high-quality speech, but iterative sampling is costly for interactive applications. We present MixiSpeech, a compact one-step student distilled from a multi-step Matcha-TTS teacher for Vietnamese–English speech synthesis. The method combines final-state, velocity, and local pair-consistency supervision under a progressive pair-stride curriculum. On FLEURS Vietnamese evaluation, MixiSpeech keeps the same 20.94M-parameter footprint and latency class as naive one-step Matcha sampling, while improving UTMOS from 1.575 to 2.264, WER from 16.22% to 12.84%, and CER from 8.15% to 5.87%.

Method Overview

MixiSpeech learns from the geometry of dense teacher trajectories rather than only a final teacher output. A frozen 64-step Matcha teacher generates trajectories offline. The one-step student is trained by a direct final-state jump, velocity matching, and pair consistency across progressively shorter teacher-state strides. At inference, the student makes a single acoustic update before a frozen HiFi-GAN vocoder renders the waveform.

Final-state consistency

One-step final estimates are directly supervised against the final teacher mel state.

Velocity matching

The student learns the local direction of the teacher’s conditional flow trajectory.

Pair consistency

Local updates align with progressively finer teacher-state pairs.

Evaluation and Limitations

Each system is evaluated on 200 Vietnamese FLEURS and 200 English VCTK prompts. UTMOS, WER, and CER are automatic diagnostics; blinded human listening results are intentionally withheld until listener collection is complete. The primary claim is the Vietnamese–English deployment setting, while VCTK is used as an out-of-domain English diagnostic.
