MixiSpeech: Progressive One-Step Distillation for Vietnamese–English Text-to-Speech

Trajectory-aware distillation for one-step speech synthesis—small, fast, and tuned for Vietnamese.

MixiSpeech Team

MixiSpeech Research · Hanoi, Vietnam

↓ Paper source◉ Live demo⌘ Method
Abstract

One acoustic update. A denser learning signal.

MixiSpeech is a compact one-step student distilled from a multi-step Matcha-TTS teacher. Rather than learning only the final sample, it learns the geometry of dense teacher trajectories through final-state, velocity, and local pair-consistency losses, under a progressive pair-stride curriculum.

Method

Distill the path—not just the destination.

A frozen 64-step teacher produces acoustic trajectories offline. The student receives an intermediate trajectory state and predicts a direct final-state jump, supervised by complementary global and local objectives. At inference, only the one-step student and a frozen HiFi-GAN vocoder are used.

What the result says

Quality recovery, without extra steps.

Against the latency-matched one-step teacher, MixiSpeech raises UTMOS from 1.575 to 2.264, reduces WER by 3.38 points, and reduces CER by 2.28 points. The 64-step teacher is 8.6× slower in the measured Vietnamese setup.

Evaluation protocol

Transparent release boundary.

Each system is evaluated on 200 Vietnamese FLEURS and 200 English VCTK prompts. UTMOS, WER, and CER are automatic diagnostics; blinded human listening results are intentionally not reported until listener collection is complete.
